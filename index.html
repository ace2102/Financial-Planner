📋 Model Assumptions & Rules
All rules baked into the model — not visible from sliders alone
Phase Structure
Phase 1 · Ages 46–54 · Accumulation
Full earned income. Mortgage + lifestyle expenses. 529 contributions active. Maximum savings rate.
Phase 2 · Ages 55–62 · Scale Back
Reduced earned income. Practice 401k rolls into IRA. Practice business sale at age 55. Practice RE income begins. 529 contributions end.
Phase 3 · Age 63+ · Retirement
No earned income. Pension + SS begin. Mortgage gone. Expenses in today's dollars inflated forward.
Expense Model
Mortgage Fixed nominal payment — never inflated. Paid off at age 63 (end of Phase 2). Slider sets the monthly amount.
Lifestyle / CC expenses Inflated at the Expense Inflation slider rate each year from age 46, peaking at age 67. After age 67, expenses decline 1% per year (retirement spending smile — less active spending in later years). Slider values are in today's dollars.
Retirement expenses Set in today's dollars on the slider. Inflated to age 63 at the inflation rate, then continues inflating to age 67, then declines 1%/yr after 67. Same spending smile applies.
Kids expense drop Applied at age 57 — reduces monthly CC/lifestyle expenses by the slider amount (kids launched, less household spending).
529 contributions End at age 55 and drop out of monthly outflows automatically.
Income Model
Wife's income growth Grows linearly from the Phase 1 slider to the Phase 2 slider across ages 46–54 (state salary step increases). Holds flat in Phase 2.
Rental income Grows at the Rental Income Growth Rate slider each year, compounding from age 46.
Practice RE income Begins at age 55. Continues until sold (if toggle is on) or indefinitely. Slider is monthly profit, not gross rent.
Pension COLA NYSLRS 2% COLA applied annually from age 63. Slider capped at 3% (NYSLRS maximum). Compounds — pension grows each year through end of projection.
One-time events Practice business sale, practice RE sale, and inheritance proceeds are all entered as post-tax net amounts — no additional tax is applied by the model. They flow directly into the portfolio.
Tax Model
Effective tax rates Three separate sliders for Phase 1, Phase 2, and Retirement. These are blended effective rates (federal + NY state combined), not marginal rates.
Mortgage deduction fade As the mortgage amortizes, the interest deduction shrinks each year. The model automatically increases your effective tax rate annually to reflect this lost deduction, using a 35% marginal rate assumption on the lost deduction amount. Effect ends at age 63 when mortgage is paid off.
Portfolio withdrawals Taxed at the current phase effective rate. Approximation assumes a blended brokerage / IRA account — LTCG rates on brokerage roughly equal blended effective rate.
Inherited IRA RMDs Taxed as ordinary income at the current phase effective rate. Tax cost is deducted from portfolio each year.
Own 401k / IRA RMDs Taxed as ordinary income at the retirement phase rate. Tax cost is deducted from portfolio. Note: at age 73 combined RMDs (~$385K/yr) plus pension, SS, and rental income will likely push marginal rate to 37% — the retirement tax rate slider should reflect this when RMDs begin.
Inherited Roth IRA (0697) Distributions are completely tax-free. No tax applied at any point. Account grows untouched and is fully distributed at age 55.
Portfolio & Withdrawal Model
Portfolio starting value Excludes 529 (tracked separately). Includes brokerage, inherited IRAs, own IRAs, 401k, and 403b. Set this to your total investable assets minus 529 balance. The inherited IRA and 401k/IRA sub-trackers show breakdowns but all are inside this number.
Withdrawals before age 55 Flat percentage of portfolio per year (the Max Withdrawal Rate slider). Used to model college cost buffer ages 50–54.
Withdrawals age 55+ Needs-based only. The model calculates all non-portfolio income (earned, rental, pension, SS, RMDs) and only withdraws from the portfolio to cover any remaining expense shortfall. If income exceeds expenses, withdrawal is zero. The Max Withdrawal Rate slider acts as a ceiling — never exceeded regardless of needs.
Investment return Single blended annual return applied to entire portfolio. No sequence-of-returns risk, no asset allocation modeling. Nominal return — not inflation-adjusted.
Retirement Account Rules
Practice 401k rollover Andrew's practice 401k ($277K) rolls into his IRA at age 55 when the practice is sold. Combined with SIMPLE IRA ($113K) for a single Andrew IRA pool.
401k contributions Andrew: $38K/year (salary deferral + profit sharing) through age 54. Sotiria: $23K/year through age 62. Contributions end when each phase ends.
RMD start age Age 73 for both Andrew and Sotiria (SECURE 2.0, born 1980). Uses IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. RMDs from all traditional IRA/401k/403b accounts are required and modeled each year.
Inherited IRA — 7861 Pre-SECURE Act (parent died Feb 2019). Stretch IRA rules apply — annual RMDs using IRS Single Life Expectancy Table, factor starting at 38.8 at age 46, decreasing by 1.0 per year. Account grows indefinitely.
Inherited IRA — 5333 Post-SECURE Act 10-year rule (parent died May 2025, was taking RMDs). Annual RMDs required ages 46–54 using Single Life Expectancy Table. Full remaining balance distributed at age 55 (year 10 deadline: Dec 31, 2035).
Inherited Roth IRA — 0697 Post-SECURE Act 10-year rule (same parent, May 2025). No annual RMDs required on inherited Roth. Let grow untouched — full distribution at age 55 (year 10 deadline). Entirely tax-free.

Purpose Reality-check the model against your actual monthly experience. Uses real take-home pay rather than gross income × (1 − tax rate).
